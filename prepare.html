Select your home country. This helps determine if a U.S. tax treaty applies to you.

Your visa type determines which tax form you file and which credits you qualify for.

Why does my visa type matter for taxes?

Your visa type determines which tax form you file. F-1 and J-1 students are usually nonresident aliens and must file Form 1040-NR, not the regular Form 1040. Filing the wrong form is one of the most common mistakes international students make.

Nonresident aliens cannot file as Head of Household or Married Filing Jointly unless specific treaty conditions apply.

Income Effectively Connected with U.S. Trade or Business

F-1 and J-1 students from certain countries may qualify for a treaty exemption. Check IRS Publication 901.

What is a tax treaty exemption?

The U.S. has tax treaties with many countries that allow certain income to be taxed at a reduced rate or not at all. F-1 and J-1 students from countries like China, India, and South Korea may qualify. Check IRS Publication 901 to see if your country has a treaty with the U.S.

Enter gain or loss from stock sales. Your 1099-B shows proceeds and cost basis.

Where do I find this on my 1099-B?

Your 1099-B from your broker shows each stock sale. Find the "Realized Gain/Loss" column. Enter a positive number for a gain and a negative number for a loss. If you sold Palantir (PLTR) shares, that gain goes here.

Deductions
Important: Nonresident aliens generally CANNOT claim the standard deduction. You must itemize deductions on Schedule A (Form 1040-NR). Exception: Students from India may claim the standard deduction under the U.S.-India tax treaty.

Credits
⚠️ Nonresident aliens on F-1, J-1, M-1, or Q visas do NOT qualify for the Earned Income Credit.
⚠️ Nonresident aliens cannot claim the American Opportunity Credit or Lifetime Learning Credit.

Nonresident aliens can only claim the Child Tax Credit if their dependent is a U.S. citizen, U.S. national, or U.S. resident alien.

Nonresident aliens may only claim the Child Tax Credit if the child is a U.S. citizen, national, or resident.

Payments

International students may receive a 1042-S for scholarship or fellowship income.

What is a Form 1042-S?

Form 1042-S is sent to nonresident aliens who received U.S.-source income like scholarships, fellowships, or income covered by a tax treaty. Your university may send you one if any of your scholarship income was taxable or covered by a treaty exemption.
